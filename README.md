What do you know about dragons? 

Well you don't need to know much, but this was the prompt from a DM to a unsuspecting group of D&D5e adventurers. 
It led to an adventure that would spawn a surge of creative storytelling and homebrewing rule sets, weapons, monsters, 
and the creative use of beans, apples, and more importantly the varied remains of the monsters and creatures these 
adventuers would defeat. When the scavaged remains of non-senient, and sentient creatures are mixed together with 
herbs, bones, or fairy poo they can be used to create the most wonderous brews known to half-orc kind.

Let me introduce you to T'chok's Magical and Wonderful Brews!!

This collection of brews have been designed for use in Foundry VTT's D&D5e Game system. Utilising Active Effects as the basis 
of applying the particular effects of drinking the brews to a player's character sheet. A DM can add these to a shop, add them as loot, or make them available to their players in whatever way they see fit. 
Once available to the players they just need to add them to their inventory, and when they want to make use of the brew they click and drink the brew. The changes to the player's ability scores are made and any new resistances or immunities are added. 
If the brew comes with a new area effect or somehting like an eye blast, these are available in their spell list. 

In the orignal game these were invented with the rule that the brew was active until a long rest or the player took a different 
brew. They didn't stack in any way. New DMs can make use of these as they see fit. The effect is on the player character sheet until they either delete the brew from their inventory or turn the effects off in the effects tab on the character sheet.

What is currently included in this module is just a small collection of the ones invented. More will be added in due course and
will prompt a major module update release. Adjustments and improvements to exiting brews will prompt a minor module update release. A complete overhaul of the design and function of the brews will prompt a step release i.e. from v1 to v2.
